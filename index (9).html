Data & Privacy

We don't store your
clients' data. Full stop.

Not a policy footnote. It's how Nodebook is architected from day one. Your client data never touches our servers and is never used to train AI.

Zero data storage

We connect via secure OAuth API. We read, process and push data in real time. Nothing is saved on our servers — ever. Your data lives in your tools, not ours.

✓ Verified architecture

AI is not trained on your data

We use the Claude API by Anthropic. Anthropic does not train their models on API usage data. Your clients' financials are never used to train any AI model.

✓ Anthropic API policy

UK GDPR & ICO compliant

Nodebook is registered with the ICO as a data processor. We sign a full Data Processing Agreement with every client. You are the data controller. Always.

✓ ICO Registered

You are the data controller. We are the data processor. Your client data lives in your Xero, QuickBooks or Sage account. Nodebook is a pipe — it processes in transit and delivers the output. We act as your technology department, not your data custodian.

Privacy Policy

Last updated: 1 March 2026 · Nodebook Systems Ltd · Registered in England & Wales

1. Who we are

Nodebook Systems Ltd is a company registered in England and Wales. We provide automation systems for UK accounting practices. We act as a data processor on behalf of accounting firms (our customers), who are the data controllers for their client data. For our own website and customer account data, we are the data controller.

Contact for privacy matters: privacy@nodebook.co.uk

2. What data we collect

Account data: Name, email address, firm name, and billing information provided when you sign up.

Usage data: Logs of automation runs and feature usage. We use this to improve the product and diagnose issues.

Client data (processed on your behalf): Financial data, contact information, and documents flowing through automations. This data belongs to you and your clients. We process it only to provide the service.

3. Legal basis for processing

We process your account and billing data on the basis of contract — necessary to provide the service you have paid for. Usage data is processed on the basis of our legitimate interests in improving the product. Client data processed through automations is handled under our Data Processing Agreement, on the basis of your instructions as data controller.

4. How long we keep data

Account data is kept for the duration of your subscription plus 12 months. Automation logs are kept for 24 months. Client data processed on your behalf is deleted within 30 days of account closure unless you request otherwise.

5. Sharing data

We do not sell your data. We share data only with sub-processors necessary to deliver the service: cloud infrastructure (AWS, UK region), email delivery (for automated reports), and accounting software APIs (Xero, QuickBooks, HMRC MTD) that you explicitly connect. A full sub-processor list is available on request.

6. Your rights

Under UK GDPR you have the right to access, correct, delete, or port your data. You may also object to processing. To exercise any right, email privacy@nodebook.co.uk. We will respond within 30 days.

7. Cookies

We use strictly necessary cookies to maintain your session. We do not use tracking, advertising, or third-party analytics cookies.

8. Changes

We will notify active customers by email of any material changes at least 14 days before they take effect.

Data Processing Agreement

Last updated: 1 March 2026

This DPA forms part of the Terms of Service between Nodebook Systems Ltd (“Processor”) and the Customer (“Controller”). It governs the processing of personal data by Nodebook on behalf of the Customer, in accordance with UK GDPR and the Data Protection Act 2018.

1. Definitions

Personal Data means any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person processed by Nodebook on behalf of the Customer, including client contact details, financial data, and any data processed through automation workflows.

Processing has the meaning given in UK GDPR Article 4(2).

2. Scope and purpose

Nodebook processes Personal Data solely to deliver the automation service as instructed by the Customer. Nodebook will not process Personal Data for any other purpose without the Customer’s written instruction, except where required by law.

3. Nodebook’s obligations

Nodebook shall: (a) process Personal Data only on documented Customer instructions; (b) ensure authorised personnel are bound by confidentiality; (c) implement appropriate technical and organisational security measures; (d) assist the Customer in fulfilling data subject rights requests; (e) delete or return all Personal Data on service termination; (f) provide all information necessary to demonstrate compliance with this DPA.

4. Sub-processors

The Customer provides general written authorisation for Nodebook to engage sub-processors. Nodebook maintains a current sub-processor list and will notify the Customer of intended changes with 14 days’ notice. Current sub-processors: Amazon Web Services (UK region — infrastructure), SendGrid (email delivery), and accounting software APIs explicitly connected by the Customer.

5. Security

Nodebook implements: encryption in transit (TLS 1.2+) and at rest (AES-256); role-based access controls; audit logs; regular security testing; and a documented incident response procedure. In the event of a personal data breach, Nodebook will notify the Customer within 72 hours of becoming aware.

6. International transfers

All Personal Data is processed within the UK and EEA. No transfers to third countries are made without appropriate safeguards (UK IDTA or equivalent).

. Why we are registered

Under the UK Data Protection Act 2018, any organisation that processes personal data must register as a data controller with the ICO. As a platform that processes financial and personal data on behalf of accounting practices, Nodebook Systems Ltd is required to maintain this registration.

. What this means for your practice

Our ICO registration covers our activities as data controller for our own business operations. For personal data we process on your behalf — your clients’ financial data flowing through automations — we act as a data processor under your instruction, governed by our DPA.

As an accounting firm, you are also required to maintain your own ICO registration as a data controller. If you have not yet registered, you can do so at ico.org.uk — it takes around 15 minutes and costs £40–60/year.
To verify our registration once confirmed, visit the ICO public register and search for “Nodebook Systems”.
